Welcome to Brno!

City for students in Czech Republic.

The etymology of the name Brno is disputed. It might be derived from the Old Czech brnie 'muddy, swampy.’Alternative derivations are from a Slavic verb brniti (to armour or to fortify) or a Celtic language spoken in the area before it was overrun by Germanic peoples and later Slavic peoples (this theory would make it cognate with other Celtic words for hill, such as the Welsh word bryn). Throughout its history, Brno's locals also referred to the town in other languages, including Brünn in German,ברין (Brin) in Yiddish and Bruna in Latin. The city was also referred to as Brunn (English: /brʌn/) in English, but this usage is not common today.
